The Probability of Winning a Lottery
Nov 22 2019   •   Chao
Fun Probability

For some time I have been reading an interesting book, in which the author analysed real-world events in our daily life from the perspective of statistics and probability. One of its topics, which no doubt relates to the two subjects, is gambling, and there is a section discussing about the strategy of playing a lottery. I have always heard that the chance of winning the top prize of a lottery is less the one of being struck by lightning (at least for the lottery held in Taiwan), so I have not bothered to pay even slight attention to it. Nonetheless, after having read that section describing a statistically reasonable approach to decide the timing of buying lottery tickets, I started wondering maybe winning a prize does not have to be all on the off chance.

The statistically reasonable approach is actually pretty simple: it is to calculate the expectation of money returned per ticket. Taking one of the major lotteries for example, the game is to pick 6 different numbers from 1 to 49, and based on how many your numbers match the randomly-drawn winner numbers, different prizes will be awarded. However, it does not mean no silver linings can be found when playing lotteries, and they lie in the prizes of which the size can grow.

For example, in the lottery mentioned above, the size of the top prize is determined without an upper bound by a certain portion of the total wager collected from selling tickets. Finally, one thing needs to be added is that the analysis discussed above only focuses on winning one particular type of prize. In terms of the true money returned on average, the calculation has to incorporate all possible prizes.
